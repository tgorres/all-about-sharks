All About Sharks

General Information

  • Group of elasmobranch fish
  • Cartilaginous skeleton
  • Five to seven gill slits on the sides of their head
  • Pectoral fins that are not fused to the head
  • Classified within the clade Selachimorpha (or Selachii)
  • Sister group to Batoidea (rays and kin)
  • Earliest confirmed modern sharks (selachimorphs) known from Early Jurassic around 200 million years ago
  • Records of true sharks may extend back as far as the Permian
  • Found in all seas
  • Common to depths up to 2,000 metres (6,600 ft)
  • Generally do not live in freshwater (exceptions: bull shark, river sharks, Ganges shark)
  • Have a covering of dermal denticles that protects skin from damage + parasites and improves fluid dynamics
  • Numerous sets of replaceable teeth
  • Several species are apex predators (at top of their food chain)
  • Since 1970, populations have been reduced by 71%, mostly from overfishing
